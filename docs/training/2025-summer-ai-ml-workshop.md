A full-day workshop on using CCAST for applied AI/ML, including basic principles of neural networks, training and fine-tuning commonly
available models, AI inference, and GPU scaling.
New for Summer 2025, the Center for Computationally Assisted Science and Technology (CCAST) is offering a free workshop on AI/ML (Artificial
Intelligence and Machine Learning). This is a full-day workshop aimed at getting you started with training, fine-tuning, and performing
inference with your own AI models using CCAST HPC resources. Topics include:

## Basics of neural networks

Different GPU models and how to leverage them
Submitting and monitoring GPU jobs on CCAST
Creating reusable Python environments
Installing popular machine learning workflows
Downloading and running publicly available AI models
Scaling up to larger models
The workshop will be held in-person at NDSU, on August 15, 2025. A detailed schedule will be made available to participants closer to the event.

## Eligibility

Registration for the Summer workshop in AI/ML is open to faculty, staff, and graduate students at NDSU only. Space is limited, and applicants
will be selected on a first-come-first-served basis.
Participants are expected to have at least some familiarity with high performance computing (HPC), either at CCAST or elsewhere. This may
include having used HPC for your own research/coursework, or participating in one or more CCAST workshops or training events.
[Register HERE](https://ndstate.co1.qualtrics.com/jfe/form/SV_2fqXtg2IwwLdX4q)
The deadline for registration is July 15.
